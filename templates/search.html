Did You Know that...

Every year, 81 pounds of clothing is thrown away by the average American. Along with water pollution and huge energy waste, the fast fasion industry is also responsible for 10% of humanity's CO2 emission. This problem makes many of us turn to thrifting - a fun and affordable way to shop for second-handed items, which can reduce waste in the manufacturing process significantly. For example, a dress purchased secondhand from the thredUp site saves 21.4 pounds of CO2 emissions -- the amount of CO2 that a mature tree can absorb in about 6 month!
